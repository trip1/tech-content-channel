## Cold open / hook

There was a period where buying a Raspberry Pi felt like trying to get concert tickets.

You did not shop. You hunted.

Rpilocator helped people do that. It tracked stock and pricing across retailers, and for a lot of makers it became the first tab you checked before starting a project.

Now it is shutting down in July.

That might be sad. It might also be a sign that the weird Raspberry Pi panic era is finally ending.

## Setup

- What we're explaining: why Rpilocator mattered and why its shutdown is a market signal.
- Why it matters: Pi availability shaped homelab and maker project choices for years.
- Viewer outcome: know what to buy now and when alternatives still make sense.

## Segment 1: why Rpilocator mattered

The Raspberry Pi used to be the default answer.

Need a tiny server? Pi.
Need a kiosk? Pi.
Need GPIO? Pi.
Need a cheap Linux box? Pi.

Then supply got weird. Prices spiked. Retailers sold out. The default answer stopped being default.

Rpilocator became useful because the market became annoying.

## Segment 2: what changed

If a stock tracker can shut down, a few things may be true:

- supply is less chaotic
- pricing is less absurd
- buyers have more alternatives
- the emergency use case is fading

That does not mean every Pi is cheap everywhere. It means the daily hunt may no longer be the main story.

## Segment 3: should you still buy a Raspberry Pi?

My rule:

Buy a Pi when you need the Pi ecosystem.

Good reasons:

- GPIO projects
- HAT compatibility
- camera/display ecosystem
- community docs
- low power projects
- educational builds

Bad reason:

- "I need a small server."

For a small server, a used mini PC can be faster, easier to cool, and sometimes cheaper once you include storage and a power supply.

## Segment 4: practical buyer guide

On screen comparison:

| Need | Better pick |
|---|---|
| GPIO/maker project | Raspberry Pi |
| Home Assistant with add-ons | Pi or mini PC |
| Docker host | mini PC |
| NAS/media services | mini PC or proper server |
| Camera project | Raspberry Pi |
| Kubernetes toy cluster | used thin clients or mini PCs |

## Result

Rpilocator going away is not just a website shutting down. It is a marker for a period when the most boring purchase in maker tech became weirdly dramatic.
