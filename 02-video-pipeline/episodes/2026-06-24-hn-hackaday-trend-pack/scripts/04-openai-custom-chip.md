## Cold open / hook

Everyone talks about the model.

The smarter move might be owning the machine that runs the model.

OpenAI is reportedly building its first custom chip with Broadcom. The interesting part is not just "AI company makes chip." The interesting part is what this says about inference, margins, and the next phase of the AI infrastructure fight.

Training got the headlines. Inference gets the bill.

## Setup

- What we're explaining: why custom AI inference chips matter.
- Why it matters: every chatbot answer, agent action, and image generation request has a hardware cost.
- Viewer outcome: understand why AI companies are moving down the stack.

## Segment 1: training vs inference

Training is the big dramatic event. Huge clusters. Giant runs. Press releases.

Inference is quieter and relentless. Every user prompt runs somewhere. Every API call burns compute. Every product feature turns into an operating cost.

If you are OpenAI-scale, shaving a little cost or latency off inference can matter a lot.

## Segment 2: why use a custom chip

A custom chip can be tuned for the workload you actually run.

Possible goals:

- lower inference cost
- better power efficiency
- predictable capacity
- less dependence on one GPU supplier
- tighter integration with internal serving systems

This does not mean GPUs go away. It means the biggest AI companies want options.

## Segment 3: why Broadcom matters

Broadcom is not the flashy name in AI GPUs, but it is deeply tied to custom silicon and networking.

For a company that needs massive inference infrastructure, the chip is only part of the system. Networking, memory bandwidth, packaging, and deployment all matter.

The AI stack is becoming vertically integrated because the generic cloud bill is too expensive at scale.

## Segment 4: what it means for builders

Most of us are not buying custom AI chips.

But this still matters because platform economics shape what developers get:

- cheaper models
- faster responses
- provider lock-in
- new API pricing
- different limits on agent workloads
- pressure on GPU cloud providers

If inference gets cheaper, products change. If inference stays expensive, products get throttled, tiered, and rationed.

## Result

The real AI story is not only which model wins benchmarks.

It is who can run useful models cheaply enough to make products work.
