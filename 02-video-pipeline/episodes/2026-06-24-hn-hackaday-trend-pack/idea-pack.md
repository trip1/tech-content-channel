# Video idea pack: HN + Hackaday trend scan, 2026-06-24

## Packaging rule for this pack

Every idea needs a human conflict, not just a link roundup. The audience should feel the problem in the first 15 seconds:

- "This tool solves a real annoyance."
- "This trend is going to hit your repo/server/workflow."
- "This weird hardware build teaches a bigger engineering lesson."

## Ranked ideas

### 1. AI PR spam is about to hit open source hard

- Source: HN story on PR spam and Greptile's OpenClaw analysis, plus related GitHub pull request limit discussion.
- Audience: developers, maintainers, AI tooling watchers.
- Hook: "Open source is getting its email spam moment. This time the spam comes as pull requests."
- Promise: explain why AI-generated contributions are different from normal low-quality PRs and what maintainers can do.
- Thumbnail: GitHub PR list drowning in red warning labels. Text: **PR SPAM IS HERE**
- Format: 8-10 minute commentary/explainer.
- Status: ready to script.

### 2. Zero-downtime Docker Compose without Kubernetes

- Source: HN story on Docker Compose zero-downtime deployments.
- Audience: homelab, indie SaaS, self-hosters, small teams.
- Hook: "You probably do not need Kubernetes just to deploy without dropping requests."
- Promise: show the mental model of blue-green Compose deploys and when it breaks.
- Thumbnail: Kubernetes logo crossed out, Docker Compose staying online. Text: **NO K8S**
- Format: tutorial/explainer with a demo.
- Status: ready to build.

### 3. Bunny DNS is free now. Should homelab people care?

- Source: HN story on Bunny DNS removing query fees and limits.
- Audience: homelabbers, creators running sites, small SaaS builders.
- Hook: "DNS bills are not supposed to be interesting. Then Bunny made theirs free."
- Promise: explain DNS pricing, what free changes, and whether you should move anything.
- Thumbnail: DNS bill at $0 next to a domain map. Text: **FREE DNS?**
- Format: practical buyer's guide.
- Status: ready to script.
